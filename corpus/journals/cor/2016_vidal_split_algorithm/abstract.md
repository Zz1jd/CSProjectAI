This paper revisits the Split decoder that converts a giant-tour permutation into feasible CVRP routes. The central claim is that the classical Bellman shortest-path formulation is not inherent to the problem: under the standard hard-capacity setting, the same decoding step can be implemented in linear time. The paper also shows how the same structural argument extends to limited-fleet and soft-capacity variants. That makes Split less of a hidden bottleneck inside route-first, cluster-second metaheuristics.
